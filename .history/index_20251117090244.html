About the Project

Building Resilient Communities

This research develops a comprehensive heat mapping framework to visualize and analyze disaster risk concentrations in Biliran Province, with specialized focus on landslides, floods, and storm surges.

By combining Geographic Information Systems, thermal remote sensing, and socio-ecological data, our system bridges the critical gap between theoretical risk models and real-world readiness.

The results provide a replicable model for other disaster-prone areas and aid in climate adaptation strategies worldwide.

Project Objectives

  • Collect and analyze geospatial data on disaster-prone areas
  • Develop interactive heat map index visualizing disaster risk levels
  • Create forecasting system to predict natural disasters
  • Evaluate system based on ISO/IEC 25010 standards
